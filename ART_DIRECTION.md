# Extraction: Dead Isles — Art Direction

## Chosen direction
**Atmospheric stylised realism**

## Intent
Dead Isles should feel moody, tropical, overgrown, tense, and believable.
The game should not read as toy-like low poly, and it should not chase ultra-realistic AAA fidelity either.

The goal is a grounded visual tone that supports:
- tropical survival ambience
- zombie tension in foliage and ruins
- environmental storytelling
- extraction-run risk and dread
- readable gameplay silhouettes

## Visual rules
Prefer:
- believable tropical island terrain and foliage
- grounded shoreline materials and weathering
- overgrowth, decay, rust, worn timber, faded paint
- readable character silhouettes
- consistent URP-friendly material response
- atmosphere, lighting, fog, and colour grading over raw polygon count

Avoid:
- obvious low-poly/cartoon shapes as the final target style
- bright toy-like environments
- heavily mismatched asset styles
- ultra-photoreal hero assets dropped into otherwise stylised scenes
- over-detailed assets that increase scope without helping mood

## Fidelity target
Aim for:
- semi-real / stylised-real environments
- game-readable props and characters
- mood-first lighting and post-processing
- medium-detail assets with strong consistency

Not required:
- AAA realism
- film-quality asset density
- highly bespoke art in early milestones

## Early milestone implication
Milestones A and B are still valid as system-first greybox milestones.
They do not need to be rebuilt from scratch.
However, future asset selection and environment dressing should now follow this art direction.

## Asset selection guidance
Choose assets that are:
- atmospheric
- tropical
- grounded
- slightly stylised but believable
- compatible with URP and Unity 6000.x workflows

Reject assets that are:
- obviously low poly
- bright/cartoon-clean
- visually incompatible with a tense survival-horror atmosphere

## Tone words
- humid
- overgrown
- tense
- decayed
- isolated
- survival-driven
- eerie
- grounded

## Summary
Dead Isles should look like a believable, moody tropical survival world with stylised restraint — not low poly, not full realism, but strongly atmospheric and consistent.
