# Extraction: Dead Isles — Opening Sequence Spec

## purpose of the opening
The opening should teach the real identity of the game in the right order:

1. survive
2. hunt
3. build a camp
4. feel temporary safety
5. discover infection
6. discover hostile humans
7. rescue the engineer
8. unlock the wider island loop

The opening should feel like a playable survival story rather than a tutorial checklist.

## target pacing
- First 10 minutes: survive and hunt
- First 20–30 minutes: establish home and meet the first zombie
- First 45–90 minutes: discover the family house, clear the gang, rescue the engineer
- After that: complete engineer setup tasks and unlock Mining Island

## phase 0 — playable hotel escape intro
The game should begin with a short playable escape from the luxury hotel during the outbreak.

### sequence goals
- show context and urgency
- keep the player in control quickly
- keep the intro short and replay-tolerable

### key beats
- alarms, smoke, panic, and visible outbreak signs
- the player moves through a short route toward the dock
- the player grabs a small practical maintenance-worker item on the way
- the player escapes by boat
- the escape ends badly with a rough landing or crash on Home Island

## phase 1 — wash ashore and immediate survival
The player wakes on or near the shoreline with almost nothing.

### starting condition
- low food
- low thirst
- slight health loss or general exhaustion
- one improvised starting tool or almost nothing useful
- no shelter

### nearby resources
The first area should quickly expose:
- sticks
- fibre
- stone
- wood or branches
- a nearby route toward fresh water or basic supplies

## phase 2 — first craft and first hunt
Before zombies are introduced, the player should be pushed into a simple hunting-and-cooking loop.

### first craftables
The player should quickly be able to craft:
- stone knife
- crude spear shortly after

### first animal encounter
The first creatures encountered should be passive animals.

Their purpose is to teach:
- observation
- crouching and careful approach
- simple hunting
- harvesting
- the need to cook meat

### first reward
Killing the animal should yield raw meat.

## phase 3 — first campfire and first home seed
Raw meat should naturally push the player into making fire.

### first placeable
- campfire

### what this teaches
- building has practical value
- cooking matters
- the player is beginning a home base, not just collecting items

### intended emotional beat
The first cooked meal should feel like the first moment of real stability.

## phase 4 — first minimal home base
After cooking, the player should be gently pushed toward minimal home setup.

### earliest home pieces
- campfire
- simple storage crate
- bedroll or sleeping spot
- optional very simple lean-to or shelter piece

The player should start to feel that this place is worth returning to.

## phase 5 — first zombie introduction
Zombies should only appear after the player has gathered, hunted, cooked, and started a camp.

### first zombie setup
- one solitary zombie
- visible before it becomes dangerous
- space for the player to observe, avoid, or engage

### what this teaches
- stealth opener bonus damage
- head versus leg targeting
- kickback for space
- infected are manageable alone, but dangerous if handled badly

## phase 6 — expanding outward from camp
After surviving the first zombie, the player becomes ready to explore farther inland.

### this exploration phase should reveal
- abandoned traces of the island family
- ruined or scavenged paths
- useful small loot spots
- signs of other humans being present

## phase 7 — family house discovery
The player should discover a larger house in the north of the island.

At first it may seem like possible shelter or survivors.
Instead, it is occupied by a hostile gang.

## phase 8 — family house conflict and engineer rescue
The player clears the gang and frees the hostage mining engineer.

This is the moment the game expands from lone survival into the first rescued-NPC progression arc.

## phase 9 — return to camp and engineer setup
The engineer is brought back to camp, then the player must:
- build him a hut
- provide basic support
- complete practical tasks
- earn the Mining Island unlock

## opening design goals to protect
The opening should make the player feel:
- stranded
- practical
- increasingly capable
- briefly safe
- then unsettled
- then responsible for something larger than themselves

## avoid
- immediate zombie swarm combat
- long non-playable exposition
- heavy tutorial popups
- base building with no practical reason
