# Extraction: Dead Isles — Family House Encounter Spec

## encounter purpose
The family house encounter is the first major human-threat escalation. It should:
- introduce hostile humans
- create the first real location-assault scenario
- contrast human enemies with zombies
- deliver the engineer rescue
- transition the player into NPC/home progression

## location identity
The family house should feel:
- larger and more developed than the player's early camp
- once comfortable and lived-in
- now dirty, looted, damaged, and occupied

It should sit in the north of Home Island as a memorable landmark.

## discovery phase
Before the player sees the gang clearly, they should notice signs such as:
- smoke
- scavenged supplies
- barricades
- voices
- dead or dragged bodies
- reinforced doors or windows

This should initially create uncertainty about whether the location is shelter or danger.

## gang identity
The gang should feel like rough survivor-opportunists rather than elite soldiers.

### weapon profile
Most of them should use:
- pipes
- clubs
- machetes
- axes
- other improvised melee weapons

There may be one limited ranged threat, but ranged pressure should remain low in the first playable.

## recommended encounter size
A good first-pass count is 4 to 6 gang members total.

### suggested spread
- 2 outside or roaming
- 2 around entry points or inside
- 1 stronger leader-type
- optional 1 lookout or ranged threat if difficulty needs it

## layout function
The location should support scouting and multiple approaches.

### recommended zones
- exterior yard with clutter and cover
- ground floor living/kitchen/hall area
- side room, shed, or garage
- a more controlled back room, upstairs room, or utility room used as the hostage space

## valid player approaches
The player should be able to:
- pick off outside enemies with stealth
- push directly and accept higher risk
- use sound and repositioning to split enemies

## human combat feel
Humans should immediately feel different from zombies.

They should:
- react faster
- investigate noise more deliberately
- reposition more intelligently
- pressure the player differently in open space

But they should still be simple enough for the first playable:
- patrol
- investigate
- alert
- chase
- basic cover use

## hostage setup
The engineer should be held somewhere believable, such as:
- a utility room
- a locked back room
- a garage or shed
- a storage room

He should be alive because the gang believes he knows something useful about the mining site.

## encounter flow
1. approach the house and scout the area
2. deal with perimeter enemies
3. breach the location
4. handle the strongest defender or leader
5. locate and rescue the engineer

## loot and reward logic
The house should give modest early rewards such as:
- food
- water
- simple meds
- scrap
- basic tools
- clues or notes

The main reward is the engineer rescue and the resulting progression.

## fail state
If the player dies during this encounter:
- they lose everything they are carrying
- the house remains uncleared
- they must regroup and try again later

## avoid
- making the gang feel like trained soldiers
- turning the house into a large tactical shooter level
- requiring advanced escort AI
- over-rewarding the location with too much gear too early
