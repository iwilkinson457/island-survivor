# Extraction: Dead Isles — First Playable Loot Table Spec

## purpose
This document defines what kinds of loot should appear in the first playable, where it should appear, and why. The goal is to keep each location distinct and reinforce progression instead of making all containers feel the same.

## loot table design rules
- loot should match location identity
- loot should support current progression needs
- high-value items should feel contextual, not random
- island-specific progression should be protected
- firearms should not randomly flood the early game

## broad loot categories
- survival supplies
- primitive crafting materials
- industrial materials
- medical items
- food and water
- documents and clues
- specialist progression items
- zombie-derived materials

## Home Island loot
### purpose
Supports early survival, hunting, camp setup, and the lead-up to the family house.

### common loot
- wood
- fiber
- stone
- small scrap
- cloth
- rope
- refillable bottles
- simple food tins in limited amounts
- bandages in very limited quantities

### natural resource loot
- animal meat
- animal hide
- sticks or branch equivalents

### low-frequency finds
- simple tools
- low-tier household scraps
- clue notes or family traces

### should not commonly appear
- iron ore
- coal
- firearms
- pistol bullets
- high-end industrial materials

## family house loot
### purpose
Represents a dangerous occupied landmark and the transition into human-conflict progression.

### common loot
- food
- water
- scrap
- cloth
- rope
- simple meds
- improvised weapons or melee upgrades

### possible special loot
- larger backpack or carry upgrade if desired
- notes, maps, or clues tied to the engineer and next progression step
- better home-use supplies than normal Home Island locations

### should not over-deliver
The family house should not become a jackpot of late-game gear.

### should not commonly appear
- random firearms in high volume
- large bullet stacks
- advanced industrial processing items

## Mining Island — worker camp and mess hall
### purpose
This is the first major expedition reward zone and should support profitable but incomplete first visits.

### common loot
- scrap
- cloth
- rope
- food tins
- bottled or stored water
- bandages and occasional small medikit
- basic industrial salvage
- worker notes, rosters, site logs, signs, and clues

### useful progression loot
- small amounts of coal-related or ore-related material
- simple components
- first meaningful industrial supplies

### should not dominate here
- large ore hauls from the safe outer camp
- easy firearm access
- large ammunition rewards

## Mining Island — storage sheds and logistics areas
### purpose
Supports resource-focused industrial reward without replacing deeper mining progression.

### common loot
- scrap
- rope
- cloth
- planks
- nails
- industrial salvage
- containers with practical expedition items

### possible useful finds
- gunpowder in limited controlled quantities if desired
- simple components for later engineering use
- clues to deeper mine sectors

## Mining Island — coal zone
### purpose
Introduces fuel and processing progression.

### common loot and resources
- coal
- mining scraps
- industrial tools or parts
- site notes or warning signs

### encounter identity
This zone should feel more exposed and industrial but not yet like the deepest terror space.

## Mining Island — iron zone / shaft access / deeper mine
### purpose
Supports higher risk and stronger later-run progression.

### common loot and resources
- iron ore
- deeper industrial salvage
- stronger site clues
- rarer useful materials

### likely support items
- more dangerous or limited medical finds
- stronger evidence of major disaster deeper in the site

### should feel
- higher risk
- tighter spaces
- greater reward tension

## zombie reward materials
### infected residue
All zombie kills should contribute infected residue value.

This can appear as:
- visible pickup that auto-collects when close enough
- a direct resource reward on kill depending on implementation

### role
Infected residue should support NPC services, unlock costs, and specialist progression alongside real materials.

## firearm and ammo loot rules
To protect progression:
- pistols should not be common random loot
- pistol bullets should not appear in large casual quantities
- firearm progression should remain tied to engineer unlocks and mining progress

## clue and progression item rules
Important non-combat items should be placed deliberately, not left to full randomness.

These include:
- engineer-related clues
- mine route information
- hotel clue/map progression items later
- logs, notes, and records tied to island identity

## container identity suggestions
### home island containers
- small sheds
- abandoned camping gear
- ruined domestic storage

### family house containers
- occupied survivor stash spots
- kitchen and bedroom storage
- gang-held supplies

### mining island containers
- worker lockers
- mess hall cupboards
- office cabinets
- storage sheds
- industrial crates

## avoid
- making all loot containers feel interchangeable
- over-randomising important progression items
- flooding the player with ammo early
- putting military-style rewards in non-military spaces
- making Mining Island outer zones more rewarding than deeper runs in every way
