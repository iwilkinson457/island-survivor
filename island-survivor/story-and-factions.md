# Story and Factions - Draft 1

Based on Wilko's current direction.

## Main long-term story direction
The strongest current direction is:
- survival while moving island to island
- searching for family

This gives the game a personal emotional hook without needing heavy cinematic storytelling.
The player is not just collecting better gear. They are following traces of missing family members across the island chain while trying to stay alive.

## Recommended story structure
### Early game goal
- survive the outbreak aftermath
- establish a home camp
- clear the starter island
- rescue the mining engineer
- unlock the first destination island

### Mid-game goal
- travel island to island
- find clues, records, objects, and survivors linked to family members
- rescue specialists who improve the camp
- build up enough capability to reach harder islands

### Personal / emotional hook
A strong idea from Wilko is that the player might find a child early.
The child is not just a passive quest marker. They behave like a real kid:
- they can be given simple tasks
- they get distracted
- they wander off
- they get into trouble
- the player has to remind them or rescue them

This could add warmth, humour, stress, and relatability to an otherwise grim world.
It also gives the base more life and helps the game feel human instead of just mechanical.

## Main enemy escalation
Enemy progression should change by stage of the game.

### Early game
Primary threat:
- zombies

Secondary threat:
- small groups of desperate survivors / raiders

### Mid-game
Primary threat increases toward:
- armed humans, especially after the player reaches the military base

Secondary threat:
- zombies remain common on many islands
- bandits / raiders can appear dynamically

### Late / harder progression
Primary high-end threat:
- mutants and special infected associated with the research centre

This creates a good escalation curve:
1. survive basic infected
2. survive armed humans
3. survive advanced outbreak consequences / mutants

## Dynamic faction interaction
A strong emergent-system idea already exists:
- raiders and bandits can spawn on any island
- they will fight zombies if they come into proximity
- they can also fight the player

This is good because it makes the islands feel active rather than stage-managed.
It also creates opportunities for stealth, scavenging, and letting enemies weaken each other.

## Home raid logic
Home base should be threatened by humans, not zombies.
This makes sense because:
- zombies cannot realistically travel in organised ways to the island
- human raiders can notice smoke, structures, lights, noise, and signs of stored value

### Raid escalation rule
Early game:
- small groups of desperate survivors
- lightly armed
- opportunistic

Later game:
- larger, more organised groups
- better equipment
- stronger intent to steal or destroy

This ties base progression directly to rising pressure.
The safer and richer the camp becomes, the more it attracts serious attention.

## NPC design direction
NPCs should be:
- light story characters
- have personality
- act as quest givers
- unlock new crafting options or services when their quests are completed

They should not require heavy survival babysitting after rescue.
Current direction:
- once rescued and housed in a small hut, they fend for themselves
- their value comes from quests, crafting, and world-building

This is a good balance because it avoids turning the game into NPC hunger-management while still making the camp feel alive.

## Current NPC roster ideas
Confirmed / likely NPC roles:
- Mining engineer
- Medic
- Gunsmith
- Farmer
- Scientist
- Technician
- Builder
- Special forces soldier / operator

## Likely role of each NPC
### Mining engineer
- unlocks mine location
- enables better ore extraction / refining knowledge
- opens early industrial progression

### Medic
- improved healing items
- treatment for infection / status effects
- better recovery items

### Gunsmith
- firearm repair
- better guns
- attachments / ammo crafting / tuning

### Farmer
- food sustainability
- crops / renewable ingredients / cooking inputs

### Scientist
- research-related crafting
- chemicals / antidotes / mutagen knowledge / advanced materials

### Technician
- electronics / power systems / batteries / radios / advanced utility stations

### Builder
- stronger structures
- better benches
- defensive improvements / base upgrades

### Special forces
- military knowledge
- tactical gear / rare weapon systems / special missions / high-threat guidance

## Tone direction
The tone should be:
- grim overall
- but with relief, personality, and bits of humour

This fits well with:
- a low-poly readable style
- casual approachability
- moments like a child NPC wandering off during chores
- personality-driven camp interactions

## Story delivery direction
Current direction suggests:
- light story
- NPC-driven quests
- not too heavy on narrative dumping

Best fit likely:
- environmental clues
- practical notes / records / found items
- short NPC dialogue and simple quest chains
- clear progression without long cutscenes

## Open story question still remaining
The family-search direction is now the best main motivation, but one important detail is still undefined:
- who exactly is the player searching for?

This matters because it changes the emotional shape of the game.
Possible options:
- spouse / partner
- child
- sibling
- parent
- small family group

There may also be a stronger hybrid if the child found early is related to this broader search.

## Design strengths currently visible
1. Clear escalation from zombies -> armed humans -> mutants
2. Home raids are human-driven and logically tied to camp growth
3. NPCs add progress and personality without becoming a management burden
4. Family search gives personal direction without needing a giant cinematic plot
5. Dynamic fights between zombies, raiders, and other enemies can create strong emergent gameplay
