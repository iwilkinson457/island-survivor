# Extraction: Dead Isles — Home Island Progression Map

## purpose
This document defines the progression structure of Home Island from crash landing through early survival, first zombie contact, family house escalation, engineer rescue, settlement growth, and onward links into Mining Island progression.

## design goals
Home Island should:
- teach the core survival loop safely but with pressure
- act as the player's long-term home and settlement space
- gradually reveal new danger layers
- support repeated return and growth
- remain useful even after new islands unlock

Home Island is not just the tutorial island. It is the emotional and structural anchor of the whole game.

## Home Island progression philosophy
Home Island should progress in stages.

The player should not experience everything at once.
Instead, the island should evolve from:
1. crash-survival space
2. primitive survival space
3. infected warning space
4. first human-threat escalation space
5. settlement-building space
6. return-and-growth space

This gives the island a long life across the game.

## stage 0 — hotel escape intro
### purpose
A short opening sequence that shows the outbreak beginning and pushes the player into escape.

### key beats
- outbreak chaos at hotel
- scramble to flee
- boat escape attempt
- crash or rough landing on Home Island

### rule
Keep this short and readable, around 5–10 minutes.
It should set tone, not become a giant opening mission.

## stage 1 — stranded survival
### purpose
Teach immediate survival basics.

### player goals
- orient themselves
- gather sticks, fibre, stone, and wood
- manage low food and water
- understand they are stranded

### key learning
The player should learn:
- gather
- craft primitive basics
- move around safely
- read environment for resources

## stage 2 — passive-animal survival phase
### purpose
Teach food, hunting, and camp setup before the infected become the main pressure.

### player goals
- find passive animals
- kill and collect raw meat
- understand food preparation need
- build first campfire

### key learning
This phase teaches:
- early hunting
- cooking
- basic camp identity
- that survival starts practical, not immediately combat-heavy

This stage is important because it creates a calmer foundation before the first zombie changes the tone.

## stage 3 — first camp setup
### purpose
Turn raw survival into the seed of a home base.

### player goals
- place and use campfire
- cook food
- possibly place storage crate
- possibly place bedroll
- possibly place simple shelter pieces

### key learning
The player learns:
- home is where survival stabilises
- building matters immediately
- extracted or carried items need a place to go later

## stage 4 — first zombie contact
### purpose
Introduce infected pressure only after the player already understands basic survival.

### rule
The first zombie should appear after the player has:
- hunted
- cooked
- established first camp functionality

### intended enemy feel
The first zombie should be:
- visible
- manageable
- avoidable if needed
- a teaching encounter for stealth, melee spacing, kickback, and head or leg targeting

### key learning
The world is no longer just harsh. It is actively dangerous.

## stage 5 — wider Home Island exploration
### purpose
Open up the island beyond immediate survival and push the player outward.

### player goals
- explore more confidently
- gather better supplies
- locate sheds, beach debris, and practical scavenging points
- begin noticing signs of greater danger inland

### key learning
The island is survivable, but it has deeper layers and threats.

## stage 6 — discovery of the family house
### purpose
Introduce the first major human-controlled danger zone.

### player observations
The family house should clearly read as:
- occupied
- dangerous
- worth investigating
- unlike normal zombie spaces

### key learning
Humans are a different kind of threat from infected.

## stage 7 — family house encounter
### purpose
Act as the first major human-combat checkpoint on Home Island.

### player goals
- prepare for assault or infiltration
- clear the gang
- survive the encounter
- secure the site
- rescue the engineer

### key learning
This is the bridge from survival into specialist- and settlement-driven progression.

## stage 8 — engineer return and settlement upgrade
### purpose
Convert combat success into Home Island growth.

### player goals
- bring engineer into camp progression
- build engineer hut
- provide support supplies
- continue camp growth

### key learning
The home base is now becoming a settlement, not just a hideout.

## stage 9 — Mining Island unlock
### purpose
Use the engineer to open the first expedition progression beyond Home Island.

### player goals
- gain trust with engineer
- unlock Mining Island destination
- prepare for off-island expedition

### key learning
Home Island is now the preparation, return, and progression hub for wider runs.

## stage 10 — return-home loop after expedition begins
### purpose
Home Island becomes the player's operational base between island runs.

### ongoing player activities
- unload loot
- craft and process materials
- expand base
- support NPCs
- prepare gear
- respond to raids
- begin deeper settlement growth

### key learning
Home Island remains relevant permanently, not just in the opening.

## Home Island region identity suggestions
### crash / shoreline zone
- first safe-ish orientation space
- light gathering and survival basics

### early camp zone
- likely place player establishes first home base
- practical build-friendly area

### beach debris and small sheds
- low-tier scavenging points
- cloth, rope, scrap, bottles, basic supplies

### wildlife / foraging spaces
- passive animals
- berries
- survival teaching spaces

### inland exploration routes
- transition into more threat-aware play

### family house zone
- first major human landmark
- first rescued specialist progression point

### later Home Island value
Even after progression expands, Home Island remains important for:
- base building
- storage
- stations
- NPC housing
- raid defense
- emotional sense of home

## Home Island danger escalation
Danger on Home Island should escalate gradually.

### early
- hunger and thirst
- gathering pressure
- simple wildlife interaction
- one or few regular zombies

### mid
- more infected pressure
- broader exploration tension
- first human-threat preparation

### later
- family house gang encounter
- occasional stronger infected types
- raid pressure tied to settlement growth

This keeps the island alive without making it overcrowded.

## Home Island progression summary
### opening flow
1. hotel escape
2. crash landing
3. gather basics
4. hunt passive animals
5. build first campfire and camp
6. first zombie encounter
7. wider exploration
8. discover family house
9. clear gang and rescue engineer
10. build engineer into settlement
11. unlock Mining Island
12. return to Home Island as long-term operational base

## key design rule
Home Island must continue feeling useful and alive after other islands unlock.
It should remain:
- the home base
- the settlement core
- the emotional anchor
- the place where progression is processed and made real

## avoid
- making Home Island feel like a disposable tutorial zone
- front-loading too many threats too early
- introducing zombies before survival basics are learned
- introducing humans before the player has enough confidence in combat and home systems
- making Home Island stop mattering after Mining Island unlocks

## summary
Home Island should work as both:
- the player's first survival map
and
- the permanent center of the game's settlement, preparation, and recovery loop

Its progression should cleanly move from survival -> home -> danger -> rescue -> settlement -> expedition hub.
