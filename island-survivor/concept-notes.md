# Concept Notes

## Your current idea in plain language
This is a cross between a survival game and an extraction game.

The player starts in a zombie apocalypse. Half the world died and the other half became zombies. The main character was a maintenance worker at a large luxury hotel on a private island. As guests and staff began turning, the player narrowly escaped by stealing a boat.

The escape does not solve the problem. The player reaches another island and runs out of fuel. This island belonged to a family, but they are zombies now. It serves as the starter island and the player's first base location.

The player begins with very basic survival: gathering fibre and sticks, crafting primitive tools, killing a few easy zombies, hunting animals, and learning the immediate environment.

Further north on the island is the family house. After the player fights through the area, they discover the house has been taken over by a gang. The gang is holding a mining engineer hostage. When the player kills the gang and frees him, he becomes the first important rescued NPC.

If the player takes him back to camp, feeds him, and builds him a wooden house, he rewards the player with the location of a mining island he had been investigating before the outbreak.

This is the gateway to the extraction structure. Each island should have something the player cannot fully replace at home. The home camp is for survival and simple crafting. Specialised islands provide advanced resources, recipes, NPCs, and equipment opportunities.

## The important progression rule
If the player wants advanced things, they must go to the correct island.

Examples:
- Basic weapons can be made locally.
- Better firearms, parts, and attachments require visiting a military island.
- Better industrial materials require a mining or industrial island.
- Specialist crafting is unlocked by rescued NPCs or location-specific stations.
