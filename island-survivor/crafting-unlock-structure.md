# Extraction: Dead Isles — Crafting Unlock Structure

## core progression rule
Crafting progression should run through four layers:

1. player basic knowledge
2. home stations
3. rescued NPC specialists
4. island-gated materials

A recipe should not unlock in isolation. It should depend on what the player knows, what stations exist at home, which NPCs are present, and whether the player has brought back the right materials from the right islands.

## layer 1 — player basic knowledge
These are the early survival recipes the player can make without needing a rescued specialist.

### available from the start or very early
- stone knife
- spear
- axe
- bow
- campfire
- bedroll
- simple bandage
- storage crate
- basic hut pieces
- wall
- door

These recipes support the early loop of gathering, hunting, surviving, starting a home camp, and reaching the family house.

## layer 2 — station-based crafting
The first playable should use a small, clear set of stations at home.

### stations in first playable
- campfire
- crafting bench
- furnace
- ammo bench

### campfire role
The campfire is the first practical station.

#### campfire recipes
- cooked meat
- simple cooked food conversion

The campfire teaches that building at home has immediate survival value.

### crafting bench role
The crafting bench is the first true upgrade station. It supports more refined manual crafting than raw inventory assembly.

#### crafting bench recipes
- machete
- refined utility crafting
- arrows or improved arrow crafting if separated from basic inventory crafting
- better storage or structure support later if needed

The crafting bench represents the camp becoming a working base rather than just a survival spot.

### furnace role
The furnace turns Mining Island resources into meaningful progression.

#### furnace recipes
- charcoal
- iron ingots

#### progression links
- wood -> charcoal
- iron ore -> iron ingots

The furnace is the bridge between mining runs and real advancement.

### ammo bench role
The ammo bench is the first dedicated firearm support station.

#### ammo bench recipes
- small pistol bullets

This station marks the point where firearms become part of the player settlement economy rather than random loot.

## layer 3 — NPC specialist unlocks
Rescued NPCs function as traders, unlock gates, service providers, and recipe enablers.

Items can require:
- materials
- infected residue
- quest completion
- the correct NPC being present

## engineer unlock role
The engineer should unlock in stages.

### engineer stage 1 — rescue and setup
After rescue and home setup quests, the engineer unlocks:
- Mining Island location
- mine access key or route knowledge
- first practical expedition guidance

### engineer stage 2 — post-mining progression
After the player brings back mining progress such as coal and iron, the engineer unlocks:
- pistol access
- ammo bench access
- bullet crafting path
- further engineering-grade services or recipes later

## engineer trade/service model
The engineer should not just hand over weapons. He should provide access in exchange for combined progression.

### example pistol logic
A pistol unlock should require:
- the engineer to be present and progressed
- quest progress
- materials such as iron ingots and gunpowder
- infected residue as part service/progression cost

## layer 4 — island-gated materials
Even if the player has the right station and the right NPC, some progress must still depend on bringing back island-specific materials.

### Home Island provides
- wood
- fibre
- stone
- animal resources
- some scrap
- the basic survival foundation

### Mining Island provides
- coal
- iron ore
- heavier industrial salvage
- deeper industrial progression materials

This ensures the player cannot fully progress by staying on Home Island forever.

## first playable unlock ladder

### stage 0 — stranded survival
Available:
- stone knife
- spear
- axe
- bow
- campfire
- bedroll
- simple bandage
- storage crate
- basic hut pieces
- walls
- door

Requirements:
- basic gathered resources only

### stage 1 — early home base
Available after settling camp:
- cooked food
- slightly improved home setup
- stable survival support

Requirements:
- campfire
- gathered resources
- hunted food

### stage 2 — engineer rescue
Available after family house arc:
- engineer at home
- engineer hut
- engineer questline
- Mining Island access and mine key/route

Requirements:
- engineer rescue
- hut built
- support tasks completed

### stage 3 — mining progression
Available after successful Mining Island runs:
- furnace becomes meaningful
- charcoal production
- iron ingot production

Requirements:
- coal
- iron ore
- furnace built
- successful extraction from Mining Island

### stage 4 — firearm progression
Available after engineer sees mining progress:
- pistol
- ammo bench
- small pistol bullets

Requirements:
- engineer progression step complete
- mining materials returned
- gunpowder
- infected residue
- likely iron ingots and other crafted inputs

## infected residue role
Infected residue should not replace normal materials. It should act as a service and progression catalyst used alongside real materials.

That means:
- real resources still matter
- stations still matter
- NPC unlocks still matter
- zombie kills remain valuable

## concise rule summary
- player crafts primitive survival items directly
- stations process food, ore, and ammunition
- engineer unlocks mine access and firearm progression in stages
- islands gate higher-value materials and progression

## avoid
- recipe overload too early
- letting one station do everything
- letting the engineer unlock too much at once
- random firearm access that breaks progression
- making island materials optional for progression
