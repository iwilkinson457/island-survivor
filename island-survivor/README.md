# Extraction: Dead Isles

Working title for a Unity low-poly survival + extraction game.

## Core premise
A maintenance worker survives the initial zombie outbreak at a luxury hotel on a private island, escapes by boat, and becomes stranded on a smaller nearby island. That first island becomes the player's base of operations. Early survival is simple and physical: gather sticks, fibre, wood, stone, hunt animals, craft primitive tools and weapons, and secure the immediate area.

After clearing a family house on the starter island, the player discovers a gang has occupied it and is holding a mining engineer hostage. Freeing him unlocks the wider progression structure: if the player shelters and feeds him, he reveals the location of a mining island. From there, each newly discovered island unlocks specialised resources, recipes, NPCs, and crafting opportunities.

The long-term loop is built around travelling between islands. The player can only craft basic items directly. Better gear, attachments, specialist tools, and advanced recipes require visiting the right island or working with rescued NPC specialists at camp.

## Current protagonist setup
- Former role: maintenance worker in a large hotel
- Starts with practical knowledge and survival improvisation, not combat expertise
- Survives outbreak by escaping hotel by boat
- Runs out of fuel and washes onto a smaller family-owned island

## Current early-game flow
1. Escape the hotel outbreak and crash-land on Home Island.
2. Gather fibre, sticks, wood, and stone.
3. Hunt passive animals and build a first campfire before the infected become the main pressure.
4. Establish a small early camp with cooking and simple shelter basics.
5. Meet the first zombie only after early survival is already understood.
6. Explore further inland and discover the family house.
7. Clear gang occupants and rescue the mining engineer hostage.
8. Bring the engineer back to camp, house him, and support him.
9. Unlock route to Mining Island.
10. First Mining Island run reaches only the worker camp and living area.
11. Recover documents, return home, and give them to the engineer.
12. Engineer provides the key or access solution for later mine entry.
