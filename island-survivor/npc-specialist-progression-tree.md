# Extraction: Dead Isles — NPC Specialist Progression Tree

## purpose
This document defines the rescued specialist structure for the game, what each specialist contributes to settlement growth, how they unlock in stages, and how their progression ties directly into islands, crafting, quests, and long-term camp development.

## design goals
Specialist NPCs should:
- feel essential rather than decorative
- unlock meaningful new capability
- remain relevant after rescue
- tie directly into island progression
- reinforce the idea that the player is building a community, not just collecting quest givers

Each specialist should act like a living branch of the game's progression tree.

## core specialist progression rule
A specialist should usually follow this structure:
1. discover or reach the specialist location
2. rescue the specialist
3. return or secure them at home
4. build or assign their hut / place in camp
5. complete trust / support tasks
6. unlock first practical service
7. unlock deeper services through follow-up quests and island returns

This keeps specialists useful over time.

## current specialist roster
Current core specialist mapping is:
- Home Island = Mining Engineer
- Mine = Builder, Medic
- Luxury Hotel = Technician, Farmer, unrelated child
- Industrial Storage = Gunsmith
- Military Base = Scientist, player's child
- Research Base = partner

This tree should be treated as the long-term specialist backbone unless later revised.

## specialist progression layers
Each specialist should unlock in layers rather than all at once.

### layer 1 — rescue value
The player gets emotional payoff and a visible new person in camp.

### layer 2 — practical settlement value
The specialist begins providing a first useful service, unlock, or progression hook.

### layer 3 — advanced progression value
The specialist opens deeper recipes, systems, islands, services, or quest chains.

This prevents rescues from feeling one-note.

## Mining Engineer
### island source
Home Island family house rescue arc

### role
The engineer is the first rescued specialist and the first true bridge between survival and expedition progression.

### initial unlock chain
1. rescue engineer from family house
2. return him to camp
3. build engineer hut
4. provide food, water, and basic medical support
5. provide basic work materials
6. gain trust
7. unlock Mining Island travel

### stage 1 value
- reveals Mining Island
- gives first major specialist trust chain
- teaches rescue -> house -> support -> unlock progression

### stage 2 value
After first Mining Island document return:
- interprets mine documents
- provides mine key or access solution
- unlocks second-run mine access

### stage 3 value
After coal and iron progression:
- unlocks pistol access
- unlocks ammo bench progression
- unlocks bullet crafting path

### long-term identity
The engineer should remain relevant through:
- practical technical interpretation
- progression to industrial systems
- site document reading
- mining-related upgrades

## Builder
### island source
Mine

### role
The builder should deepen home construction and structural progression.

### likely stage 1 value
- unlocks stronger building options
- improves hut and wall progression
- enables better home layout growth

### likely stage 2 value
- unlocks stone building or stronger structural pieces later
- improves defenses and repair options
- supports larger settlement expansion

### long-term identity
The builder should own structural camp evolution.

## Medic
### island source
Mine

### role
The medic should expand healing and survival recovery.

### likely stage 1 value
- better healing item support
- improved medical crafting or treatment
- stronger recovery options after hard runs

### likely stage 2 value
- better medikits
- support against later harsher enemy pressure
- biological or infection-related support later if needed

### long-term identity
The medic should make recovery and resilience feel more sophisticated.

## Technician
### island source
Luxury Hotel

### role
The technician should bridge technical systems and later utility progression.

### likely stage 1 value
- repair-oriented or tool-oriented services
- support for more advanced gear or site access logic

### likely stage 2 value
- later utility systems
- electronics or access-related progression
- support for industrial and technical islands

### long-term identity
The technician should become a practical systems expert for later-game settlement capability.

## Farmer
### island source
Luxury Hotel

### role
The farmer should deepen camp sustainability.

### likely stage 1 value
- better food production support
- improved farm plot usefulness
- more stable Home Island food loop

### likely stage 2 value
- stronger sustainable food systems
- higher-value crop or settlement food progression

### long-term identity
The farmer should help the settlement feel self-sustaining rather than permanently scavenger-dependent.

## Unrelated child survivor
### island source
Luxury Hotel

### role
Primarily emotional and settlement-tone driven, not a heavy systems specialist.

### progression value
- adds emotional weight to the settlement
- raises stakes of protection and survival
- helps reinforce the broader human side of the world

### later narrative value
This child becomes more important after the player's own child is found, helping reinforce family and community themes.

## Gunsmith
### island source
Industrial Storage

### role
The gunsmith should become the deeper firearms and weapon-support specialist.

### likely stage 1 value
- expands firearm access beyond early pistol stage
- improves weapon maintenance or upgrade options

### likely stage 2 value
- unlocks broader ammo or firearm services
- improves ranged combat progression and weapon quality

### long-term identity
The gunsmith should own serious weapon progression after the engineer has introduced the first firearm layer.

## Scientist
### island source
Military Base

### role
The scientist should bridge advanced clues, dangerous research interpretation, and later mutant or research-base progression.

### likely stage 1 value
- interprets scientific materials
- unlocks research-oriented quests or understanding

### likely stage 2 value
- supports endgame threat knowledge
- unlocks deeper research-base progression
- helps explain advanced infected or mutation systems

### long-term identity
The scientist should be a key late-game knowledge gatekeeper.

## Player's child
### island source
Military Base

### role
Primarily emotional and narrative, but also central to the game's long-term motivation.

### progression value
- major emotional milestone
- strengthens settlement meaning
- changes the camp's emotional tone

### later interaction
The player's own child can later help contextualise or support the unrelated child, strengthening the sense of found family and surviving community.

## Partner
### island source
Research Base

### role
Final major emotional objective.

### narrative outcome
Current intended reveal is that the partner is dead at the end.

### design value
The partner is still a major motivator throughout the whole game and helps frame the final emotional payoff and cost of the journey.

## specialist progression rules
### rule 1
No specialist should unlock everything immediately after rescue.

### rule 2
Every specialist should provide at least one practical reason to revisit them after rescue.

### rule 3
Each specialist should feel tied to their own role and island identity.

### rule 4
Settlement growth should be visible through specialist presence, services, and changing utility.

### rule 5
A specialist's best rewards should usually come from a short trust chain and follow-up tasks, not instant rescue completion.

## early-game specialist arc
The first playable should prove the specialist structure mainly through the engineer.

### first-playable specialist loop
- rescue engineer
- house engineer
- support engineer
- unlock Mining Island
- return with documents
- unlock mine access
- return with mining outputs
- unlock pistol and ammo support

If this works, the whole specialist system works.

## long-term specialist philosophy
The camp should gradually transform from:
- one survivor barely scraping by
into
- a rough but growing community of people with skills, needs, and practical value

Specialists are one of the main systems that make this transformation believable.

## avoid
- rescuing specialists who become passive decoration
- specialists who unlock everything at once
- specialists whose services do not match their role
- too many overlapping specialist roles with no clear ownership
- camp growth that feels cosmetic rather than functional

## summary
The specialist progression tree should create a layered community structure where:
- the engineer unlocks expedition and early technical progression
- the builder unlocks stronger construction
- the medic unlocks stronger recovery
- the technician unlocks technical systems
- the farmer unlocks sustainability
- the gunsmith unlocks deeper weapon progression
- the scientist unlocks advanced research understanding
- the children and partner carry emotional long-term stakes

This makes the settlement itself one of the game's main progression systems.
