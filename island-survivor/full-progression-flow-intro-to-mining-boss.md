# Extraction: Dead Isles — Full Progression Flow from Intro to Mining Island Boss

## purpose
This document defines the intended end-to-end early-game progression slice, starting from the hotel outbreak intro and carrying through to the Mining Island boss milestone. The goal is to give the project one clean, playable progression arc that proves the game's core identity.

## design goals
This progression slice should prove:
- survival and home-building work
- zombies and human enemies feel distinct
- rescued specialists drive progression
- extraction runs matter
- islands are cleared in stages, not one raid
- Mining Island provides the first major expedition arc

This slice should feel like a complete early game, not just disconnected systems.

## high-level progression ladder
1. hotel outbreak intro
2. crash landing on Home Island
3. primitive survival and first camp
4. first zombie encounter
5. wider Home Island exploration
6. family house discovery and assault
7. engineer rescue and settlement integration
8. Mining Island unlock
9. first Mining Island run — camp only, mine locked
10. return documents to engineer
11. second Mining Island run — mine opens
12. deeper Mining Island progression
13. Mining Island boss milestone
14. return home with major progression gain

## phase 1 — hotel outbreak intro
### purpose
Set tone, establish urgency, and force escape.

### player experience
- outbreak begins in the hotel
- immediate confusion and danger
- player flees rather than settles in
- escape attempt by boat
- crash or rough arrival on Home Island

### success condition
The player reaches Home Island alive.

### design rule
Keep this phase short, roughly 5–10 minutes.
It should establish tone, not become a long mission.

## phase 2 — stranded survival on Home Island
### purpose
Teach the most basic survival actions.

### player goals
- gather sticks, fibre, stone, and wood
- find food and water pressure points
- understand immediate vulnerability

### systems proven
- basic gathering
- primitive crafting
- environment reading
- movement and resource collection

## phase 3 — passive-animal hunting and first camp
### purpose
Teach survival before the infected dominate the tone.

### player goals
- encounter passive animals
- gather raw meat
- build campfire
- cook first food
- optionally place storage crate, bedroll, or simple shelter pieces

### systems proven
- hunting
- cooking
- first home setup
- practical survival stabilisation

## phase 4 — first zombie encounter
### purpose
Introduce infected threat after basic survival is understood.

### player goals
- survive or avoid the first zombie
- learn stealth opener, melee spacing, kickback, and head/leg damage concepts

### systems proven
- zombie pressure
- basic combat feel
- readable enemy difference from animals and world hazards

## phase 5 — wider Home Island exploration
### purpose
Expand the player's confidence and push them beyond immediate camp survival.

### player goals
- loot sheds, beach debris, and survival points
- gather better practical resources
- explore inland with caution
- notice signs of deeper danger

### systems proven
- exploratory scavenging
- basic route knowledge
- early prep-before-risk mentality

## phase 6 — family house discovery
### purpose
Reveal the first major human-threat location.

### player goals
- observe signs of gang occupation
- recognise the location as important and dangerous
- prepare before engaging

### systems proven
- environmental storytelling
- threat reading
- deliberate combat planning

## phase 7 — family house assault and engineer rescue
### purpose
Bridge survival gameplay into specialist-driven progression.

### player goals
- clear the gang threat
- survive close-quarters human combat
- find and rescue the engineer
- secure the location

### systems proven
- first human encounter structure
- distinction between zombies and humans
- meaningful location clear
- first specialist rescue payoff

### outcome
The engineer becomes the first true settlement specialist.

## phase 8 — engineer camp integration
### purpose
Turn rescue into ongoing progression.

### player goals
- bring engineer into camp progression
- build engineer hut
- provide food, water, bandage, and work materials
- earn trust

### systems proven
- NPC rescue matters after combat
- Home Island grows through specialist support
- progression is tied to helping people, not just looting

### outcome
Mining Island unlocks as the first major expedition destination.

## phase 9 — first Mining Island run
### purpose
Teach that islands are not meant to be fully cleared in one trip.

### player goals
- reach Mining Island worker camp and living area
- scout the area
- find useful supplies
- discover the mine gate is locked
- recover documents explaining or implying access requirements
- extract safely

### systems proven
- extraction-run structure
- partial expedition success
- clue recovery instead of full clear
- island staging

### key rule
The player must not enter the actual mine on the first run.

## phase 10 — return documents to engineer
### purpose
Make the specialist practically useful and reinforce return-home progression.

### player goals
- bring documents back to Home Island
- give them to the engineer
- receive explanation and access solution

### systems proven
- NPC interpretation matters
- home is where progress is processed
- extracting with information is as valuable as extracting with materials

### outcome
Engineer provides the key or access solution to open the mine.

## phase 11 — second Mining Island run
### purpose
Open the next progression layer.

### player goals
- return to Mining Island
- unlock and pass through the mine gate
- begin early mine progression
- gather first meaningful mining resources
- extract safely

### systems proven
- staged expedition structure
- progression through revisit
- first real industrial resource loop

## phase 12 — deeper Mining Island runs
### purpose
Escalate value, danger, and depth.

### player goals
- push deeper into coal and then iron progression
- face stronger infected combinations
- recover better resources and dangerous clues
- build toward the boss milestone

### systems proven
- repeated island mastery
- deeper risk/reward
- stronger extraction tension
- greater need for preparation and home return

## phase 13 — Mining Island boss milestone
### purpose
Deliver the first true island-ending combat milestone.

### player goals
- reach the deeper boss zone
- defeat the mutated miner-type boss
- survive the fight and extract with the rewards

### systems proven
- boss encounter structure
- culmination of Mining Island mastery
- meaningful milestone reward after multi-run buildup

### expected reward type
- major progression confirmation
- stronger materials or progression item
- next-island or next-phase clue
- major specialist follow-up value back at home

## phase 14 — return home after boss
### purpose
Cash out the arc and convert expedition success into settlement and future progression.

### player goals
- unload loot and clues
- process materials
- unlock next services or follow-up specialist progression
- prepare for the next major island arc

### systems proven
- home remains central
- boss kills are not endpoints on their own
- progression only becomes real once successfully brought back

## player learning across the full slice
By the end of this full early-game flow, the player should understand:
- survival starts practical
- home matters
- zombies and humans are different threats
- rescuing specialists changes the game
- extraction is mandatory for keeping progress
- islands are staged and revisited
- home is where expedition progress becomes long-term growth

## minimum systems this slice proves
This slice should validate:
- first-person movement
- gathering and primitive crafting
- cooking and simple survival
- base placement and camp growth
- zombie combat
- human combat
- NPC rescue
- specialist trust chain
- island unlock
- extraction loop
- locked-and-revisit island structure
- boss progression milestone

## pacing summary
### early tone
survival, uncertainty, learning the island

### middle tone
danger escalation, first human conflict, first specialist rescue

### later early-game tone
structured expeditions, repeated mining runs, bigger rewards, first real boss milestone

## avoid
- compressing this whole arc into too few hours or too few runs
- letting the player fully clear Mining Island on run one
- making the family house feel optional or low-value
- making the engineer a one-step unlock only
- making the boss feel disconnected from the staged expedition structure

## summary
The full early-game slice should flow like this:

hotel outbreak -> crash -> survive -> hunt -> camp -> first zombie -> explore -> family house -> rescue engineer -> build trust -> unlock Mining Island -> first run documents only -> engineer unlocks gate -> deeper mine runs -> boss -> return home stronger

That is a clean and convincing proof-of-concept arc for the game.
